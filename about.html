Hi,

My name is Yedong Sh-Chen.

I study games, digital media, and modern China.

I'm completing a Ph.D. at Harvard.

My dissertation, "Chinese Video Games: World, Object, and Artificial Intelligence," provides the first archive-based history of digital gaming in China since the 1970s, addressing key theoretical issues including world-building, artificial affect, AI ethics, and the politics of play, gender, and pleasure.

I'm also working on a side project about the history of computer graphics in East Asia.

In my spare time, I help the Harvard-Yenching Library collect games.

I used to be a stage actor in China and off-Broadway.

Now I live in Cambridge with my partner who's running a cool startup.
